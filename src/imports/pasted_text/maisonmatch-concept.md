You are an elite creative director, brand strategist, and senior frontend designer. I want you to generate a polished website concept and frontend implementation for my business, MaisonMatch.

Business name: MaisonMatch
Tagline: Every piece finds its match
Business type: AI-powered outfit recommendation and clienteling platform for independent fashion boutiques.

MaisonMatch helps independent boutiques increase basket size and customer retention by turning a single item into a complete shoppable outfit. A customer scans a QR code on an item and instantly receives outfit suggestions built from the boutique’s live inventory. After purchase, MaisonMatch remembers what each customer owns and uses that “wardrobe memory” to recommend new arrivals that match their past purchases.

I want the website to feel like an editorial fashion magazine crossed with a refined SaaS landing page. It should feel fresh, cool, understated, premium, and boutique-focused. Do not make it look like generic AI startup slop. Avoid the usual overused gradients, floating blobs, neon AI visuals, random 3D shapes, cliché dashboard mockups, and cluttered sections.

The design direction should be:

* Editorial fashion magazine aesthetic
* Minimal but not boring
* Luxurious but not flashy
* Understated, intelligent, and stylish
* Boutique, not corporate enterprise
* Clean typography with strong hierarchy
* Lots of whitespace
* Sharp art direction
* Asymmetrical layouts where appropriate
* Beautiful section titles and short, confident copy
* A refined palette: off-white, warm black, soft stone, muted taupe, deep espresso, maybe one subtle accent color
* Use photography-inspired placeholders or elegant product-card compositions rather than generic tech illustrations
* Make the AI feel invisible, tasteful, and practical, not gimmicky

Website pages/sections needed:

1. Home
2. Solution
3. How It Works
4. About Us
5. Request a Demo

Core messaging:
MaisonMatch gives independent boutiques enterprise-level personalization without forcing them to become technology companies. It works with their existing inventory, POS, and product catalog to help them sell more complete outfits and build stronger customer relationships.

Key features to explain:

1. Outfit Completion
   Customers scan or select one item and MaisonMatch generates two to three complete shoppable outfits using in-stock products from the boutique.

2. Wardrobe Memory
   After a customer buys something, MaisonMatch remembers what they own and later matches new arrivals to those past purchases. Boutique owners can send personalized messages like: “This new hoodie pairs perfectly with the jeans you bought last month.”

3. Boutique-Friendly AI
   No app download for customers. No product re-shoots. Minimal staff training. Works with Shopify, Square, Lightspeed, or existing inventory systems.

4. Owner Curation
   Boutique owners can approve, reject, or refine recommendations so the system respects the boutique’s taste and brand identity.

5. Sales Impact
   The platform is designed to increase average transaction value, items per transaction, repeat purchase rate, and clienteling response rate.

Technology should be referenced tastefully, not overwhelmingly:
MaisonMatch uses visual embeddings, fashion logic, and learned compatibility to recommend outfits based on color, texture, silhouette, occasion, customer history, and boutique inventory. Do not make the website too technical. Mention the AI only where useful.

Suggested site structure:

Home:

* Strong editorial hero section
* Headline should feel like a fashion publication, not a SaaS cliché
* Include tagline “Every piece finds its match”
* Include a short explanation of what MaisonMatch does
* CTA buttons: “Request a Demo” and “See How It Works”
* Include a tasteful visual concept, maybe a boutique product grid transforming one item into a complete outfit

Solution:

* Explain the core problem: boutiques lose revenue when customers buy one piece instead of a full outfit
* Explain why larger retailers have personalization advantages
* Position MaisonMatch as lightweight personalization built for independent boutiques
* Include sections for Outfit Completion and Wardrobe Memory

How It Works:
Use a clear four-step flow:

1. Connect inventory
2. Generate outfit pairings
3. Launch QR codes in-store or online
4. Track sales impact and repeat purchases

About Us:

* Make this feel human and brand-led
* Explain that MaisonMatch was created for independent boutiques that want the intelligence of modern retail without losing their personal, curated identity
* Avoid sounding fake, overly grandiose, or corporate
* Tone should be thoughtful, confident, and fashion-aware

Request a Demo:

* Include a clean demo request form
* Fields: name, boutique name, email, website or Instagram, POS platform, short message
* CTA: “Request a Demo”
* Add a short reassuring line: “Built for boutiques. No setup fee for pilot partners.”

Copywriting rules:

* Avoid generic phrases like “revolutionizing retail,” “seamless experience,” “unlock the power of AI,” “cutting-edge technology,” “game changer,” or “future of fashion.”
* Keep copy short, elegant, and specific.
* Use confident editorial language.
* The site should sound like it belongs to a fashion-tech company with taste.

Design rules:

* Do not overcrowd the page.
* Use strong typography and spacing.
* Use thin borders, large margins, editorial captions, product-style cards, and elegant hover states.
* Avoid excessive animations. Any motion should be subtle and premium.
* Make the site responsive and polished on desktop and mobile.
* Use realistic placeholder content where needed.
* Use accessible contrast and clean navigation.

Technical output:
Generate a complete modern frontend implementation using React and Tailwind CSS.
Use component-based structure.
Make the code clean, readable, and easy to edit.
Use no unnecessary dependencies unless truly needed.
Include all sections/pages in a polished single-page or multi-section landing page experience with navigation anchors.
Make it feel production-ready, not like a template.

Final goal:
Create a website that feels like a boutique fashion magazine quietly became a smart retail technology company. The result should be elegant, memorable, modern, and specific to MaisonMatch.
